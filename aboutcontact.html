About the Recipe Hack Machine

A little over a decade ago, I was forced to eliminate gluten, dairy, soy, corn, and oats from my diet. While there were many great recipes and cookbooks out there, it took time and a lot of research to be able to convert "regular" recipes to work without the ingredients I can't have. I've created the shortcut that I wish I had. It's hard enough living with food allergies/sensitivities - hopefully this will make it a little easier.

In any reasonable universe, if you couldn't have wheat flour, whatever. Put an equal amount of rice flour in there and call it done. In our universe, that results in outcomes that are somewhat less than... food. Wheat, milk, and eggs have chemical properties that perform critical functions in our baked goods. We can't just replace the original with an equal volume of something that seems similar. We have to mimic the ways the original ingredient contributed to the whole in terms of staying moist, holding together, and having the right texture. Usually that means accounting for changes in the amount of liquid, protein, fat. Sometimes we also add a little something to boost the taste or color. It's half math, half art, half wizardry, and nobody should have to figure that kind of thing out while they're riding out the symptoms of their last gluten poisoning. So here is the Recipe Hack Machine to reduce the number of steps between you and some actually good baked goods!
